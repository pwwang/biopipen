  • fmlfile — The formula file. The first column is grouping and thesecond column is the formula. It must be tab-delimited.

...
Sm       1    0    0          0

    Sample   F1   F2   F3   ...   Fn
S1       1.2  3.4  5.6        7.8
S2       2.3  4.5  6.7        8.9
...
Sm       5.6  7.8  9.0        1.2

  • outfile — The output file. It is a tab-delimited file with the firstcolumn as the grouping and the second column as the p-value.

Gk     Fn ~ F3       0.123   2       1    0.123   0.123  0.123 0.123

Input
  • fmlfile — The formula file.
    Case   M   Y   X   Cov     Model_M    Model_Y
Case1  F1  F2  F3  F4,F5   glm        lm
...
    Where Y is the outcome variable, X is the predictor variable, M is the mediator variable, and Case is the case name. Model_M and Model_Y are the models for M and Y, respectively. envs.cases will be ignored if this is provided.
  • infile — The input data file. The rows are samples and the columns arefeatures. It must be tab-delimited. 
    Sample   F1   F2   F3   ...   Fn

...
Sm       5.6  7.8  9.0        1.2
Output
  • outfile — The output file.Columns to help understand the results: Total Effect: a total effect of X on Y (without M) (Y ~ X). ADE: A Direct Effect of X on Y after taking into account a mediation effect of M (Y ~ X + M). ACME: The Mediation Effect, the total effect minus the direct effect, which equals to a product of a coefficient of X in the second step and a coefficient of M in the last step. The goal of mediation analysis is to obtain this indirect effect and see if it's statistically significant.

  • method (choice) — The method used to calculate the meta-pvalue.
    • - fisher: Fisher's method.
    • - sumlog: Sum of logarithms (same as Fisher's method)
    • - logitp: Logit method.
    • - sumz: Sum of z method (Stouffer's method).
    • - meanz: Mean of z method.
    • - meanp: Mean of p method.
    • - invt: Inverse t method.
    • - sump: Sum of p method (Edgington's method).
    • - votep: Vote counting method.
    • - wilkinsonp: Wilkinson's method.
    • - invchisq: Inverse chi-square method.
  • na — The method to handle NA values. -1 to skip the record. Otherwise NAwill be replaced by the given value.
  • padj (choice) — The method for p-value adjustment.
    • - none: No p-value adjustment (no Padj column in outfile).
    • - holm: Holm-Bonferroni method.
    • - hochberg: Hochberg method.
    • - hommel: Hommel method.
    • - bonferroni: Bonferroni method.
    • - BH: Benjamini-Hochberg method.
    • - BY: Benjamini-Yekutieli method.
    • - fdr: FDR correction method.
  • pval_col — The column name used in in.infile as p-value column.
